## Five Perspectives on the Substrate

### 1. Information-Theoretic Perspective: **The Substrate Is Information**

**Claim**: Semantic substrate = Pure information (distinctions, bits, mutual information)

#### Information as Foundation

From Shannon's information theory:
- **Information = Reduction of uncertainty**
- **Entropy H = -Σ p(x) log p(x)** - Expected information content
- **Mutual Information I(X;Y)** - Shared information between systems

**Semantic primaries are patterns in information space**:

- **Love (L)**: Mutual Information I(X;Y)
  - How much knowing X tells you about Y
  - Connection strength between entities
  - **Love is information sharing**

- **Justice (J)**: Constraint satisfaction
  - How many constraints are satisfied / total constraints
  - Structural integrity in information space
  - **Justice is information structure**

- **Power (P)**: Channel capacity
  - Maximum information transmittable per time
  - Bandwidth of system
  - **Power is information throughput**

- **Wisdom (W)**: Processing efficiency
  - Information extracted / resources consumed
  - Compression ratio of knowledge
  - **Wisdom is information optimization**

#### The Substrate: Distinctions

**At the deepest level**: Information requires **distinctions**.

- Before distinction: Undifferentiated unity (0 information)
- After distinction: 0 vs 1 (1 bit of information)
- All information built from binary distinctions

**Gregory Bateson**: "Information is a difference that makes a difference."

**The substrate is the capacity for distinction itself.**

Without the ability to distinguish:
- No 0 vs 1
- No true vs false
- No this vs that
- No information
- No semantics
- No LJPW

**Implication**: Semantic substrate = **The primordial capacity to make distinctions**.
